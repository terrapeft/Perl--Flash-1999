Julie Bowen 

"The Last Man On Planet Earth" - UPN 

     Fans may recognize the beautiful Julie Bowen from her starring role on The WB's mod-squad-like action show "Three." While that series had a short life, Bowen has kept busy with guest stints on "ER," "Party of Five," "Acalpulco H.E.A.T." and "Strange Luck." Now she's back, starring in UPN's sci-fi thriller "The Last Man on Planet Earth." 

 What drew you to "The Last Man On Planet Earth?" 

I haven't really had that many opportunities to play "lead" so I guess I jumped at the chance. I have also never done any "sci-fi" projects and thought it might be fun. 

Tell us about your part and what you liked best about working on this project. 

I play a scientist in a futuristic world in which 99% of the men have been wiped out. As a result, the women are nearly all homosexuals and the children are cloned. My character is determined to create a man (she's a closet heterosexual). Frankly, I thought the idea was fun and sort of campy, but it also raises some interesting questions. What would the world be like without any men? What would it be like if heterosexuals were discriminated against? 

"Three" developed of a strong fan base who fought for it. What kind of response did you get from fans? 

Did they really fight for it? Wow! I'm flattered. I guess I'm not that aware of such a big fan base. I have a few core people who write me no matter what I'm doing, but I hardly have sacks of mail being dropped on my door! The people who support me seem to like it when I play sassy and "un-girly" characters. Just as a footnote: Fans are good. 

Why do you think the series didn't catch on... do you think it was given enough time to develop? What would you like to say to your fans? 

Hmmmmm. I think the series debuted on a network (the WB) that was in the process of finding its audience. With the arrival of "Dawson's Creek" - which debuted at the same time "Three" did - I think they figured out their demographic pretty quickly. Clearly audiences wanted something youger and more reality based. "Three" was fun, but it is simply a different genre. Maybe if they had left it on longer it would have had greater success, but you can't mandate what people watch. Besides, that Pacey kid is cute. Who am I to argue? 

What are some of your favorite TV shows? 

I am a big fan of the HBO series "OZ" and "The Sopranos." They aren't exactly family viewing, but there is nothing like them on TV. On the lighter side, you just can't beat "The Simpsons" and "South Park." 

One thing people don't usually know about me is: 

I read books more than I go out. As a matter of fact, I get a little concerned about some of my anti-social habits. I will choose a night with Somerset Maugham or Russell Banks over a crowded bar any day. 

What is the best showbiz advice you ever got? 

It's a war of attrition. If you have patience and a modicum of faith in yourself your chances are not too bad. 

What other projects do you have coming up? 

Maybe a bit more on E.R. but basically I'm hunting for my next job. 

To this point, what was your favorite guest spot on a show and why? 

I've loved doing "E.R." for the quality of the writing and the great people I get to work with. Strangely, however, my favorite guest spot was probably my first "Class of '96." It was the first time I knew definitively that I wanted to keep acting. 

What is the most recent CD you bought?

Although I am not a big country music fan, Willie Nelson's "TEATRO" is insanely good. You don't have to like country to love this album.
